NASA Astronomy Picture of the Day

January 15, 2024

This image captures the majestic spiral galaxy NGC 1055 in stunning detail. Located about 60 million light-years away in the constellation Cetus, this edge-on galaxy reveals intricate dust lanes and star-forming regions. The image was created using data from the Hubble Space Telescope and showcases the galaxy's extended disk, distorted by gravitational interactions with its neighbor, the famous Messier 77 galaxy.

About Our Club

Founded in 2010, the Hawking Astronomy Club is dedicated to fostering a love for astronomy and space science among people of all ages. We believe that understanding our universe is not just for professional scientists but for everyone who looks up at the night sky with wonder.

Our club organizes regular stargazing events, lectures by renowned astronomers, workshops on astrophotography, and community outreach programs to share the beauty of the cosmos.

Whether you're an experienced astronomer or just starting your journey into space exploration, you'll find a welcoming community of like-minded enthusiasts at our club.
